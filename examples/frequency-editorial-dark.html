"You don't have a marketing problem. You have a discovery problem, and you've been told to solve it with a media kit." The premise

There are three ways a sponsor finds a show. Two of them cost you money.

The first way is through an agency, which is fine if your show already prints money, and disappointing if it doesn't. The second is through a network, which will happily take twenty-five percent of your rate and then ask you to say the word "unfortunately" less. The third way is the one nobody writes down. A sponsor looks at their existing customers, finds three podcasts those customers already subscribe to, and sends an email to whoever answers first.

Frequency writes down the third way. We build a public index of independent shows, tagged by what their audience actually buys, and let sponsors filter it the way they filter everything else. When someone browses for a show like yours, you are in the list. When they open your card, you have a rate to quote and a pitch to send. That is the whole product.

You get a rate card, one pitch template, and a list of interested brands. The rest is you.

Frequency is not a marketplace. There is no bid button. No automated matching. No AI writing your pitch, because the pitch is the only interesting part. What Frequency does is what you shouldn't have to do yourself. It puts your show on the map that a brand's growth marketer already reads, with the data she was going to ask for anyway. From there, the two of you talk like adults.

The founders you'll meet on the platform are running independent shows because they wanted to keep the ownership. That is the whole reason we exist. If we could make it more efficient than that, we would. We haven't found a way that doesn't take some of the ownership back.
