Chapter 01

There are three ways a sponsor finds a show. Two of them cost you money.

"The third way is the one nobody wrote down. So we wrote it down."

The first way is through an agency, which is fine if your show already prints money and disappointing if it doesn't. The second is through a network, which will take twenty-five percent of your rate and then quietly ask you to say the word "unfortunately" less.

The third way is the one nobody writes down. A sponsor looks at their existing customers, notices three podcasts those customers already subscribe to, and sends an email to whoever answers first.

Frequency writes down the third way. We publish an index of independent shows, tagged by what their audience actually buys, and let brands filter it the way they filter everything else. When someone browses for a show like yours, you are in the list. When they open your card, you have a rate to quote and a pitch ready to send. That is the whole product.
